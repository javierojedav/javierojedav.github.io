Hola a todas y todos! This is my research site

My full name is Javier Alejandro Ojeda Vargas, but I sign my papers as Javier Ojeda.

I am a geophysicist holding a Ph.D. in Earth and Environmental Sciences. My research focuses on using geodetic and seismological tools to explore a wide range of active tectonic and non-tectonic processes, such as earthquakes, transient deformation in subduction zones and active fault systems, strong ground motions, and the impact of seismic hazards on society. Such amount of work has only been possible thanks to the generosity of great researchers who have guided me thus far, among them Jean-Mathieu Nocquet (IRD, IPGP), Sergio Ruiz (DGF), Fabian Bonilla (IPGP), Aybige Akinci (INGV), Hajime Shiobara (ERI), Christophe Vigny (ENS), and Roland Bürgmann (UC Berkeley). More recently, I have been working on understanding the interaction between fast and slow earthquakes, particularly in Chile, taking advantage of seismological and GPS networks.
